Executive Summary

For this project, our team was prompted to apply IoT technology to address a specific opportunity around citizen engagement in the United States. Specifically, we tackled the problem around voting rights by providing a transparent and efficient solution for long lines. We believe this particular problem would see the greatest improvement with the implementation of an IoT solution.

Role

UX Designer

Duration

4 Weeks

Team

Amanda Piccolini, Cara Chen, Shaobo Zhang

Domain Research

Because voting is such a complex problem space, to help me break down the problem into a more digestable scope, I first started listing keywords from the voting process.

By listing out the keywords, I was able to draft an amateur sentence, which provides a starting point for the concept map.

Taking advantage of one’s right to vote can be a complex process, influenced by government agencies, individuals, environmental, institutaional, and sociatal factors.

Storyboards

We then narrowed down to three significant problem areas where we felt our intervation could be most impactful: voter motivation, mail-in voting transparency, and long waiting times. We further explored each area, brainstorming a solution for each problems through story boarding.

The IoT solutions we explored ranged from geo-based information hubs and mail-in tracking systems to sensors and line estimation devices. We ultimately landed on the problem space of voter lines because we believe this particular problem would see the greatest improvement with the implementation of an IoT solution. This then led to our problem statement:

How can we provide a TRANSPARENT and EFFICIENT solution for LONG LINES?

Peototype Design

For this project, I designed a set of web-app prototypes to help demonstrate how the user will interact and benefit from our solution.

Concept Video

Besides designing the web-app prototype, I was also responsible for drawing the visual scripts and gathering visual assets required for the concept video.

Visual & Scripts

When developing our visuals and scripts, there were three main points that I wanted to illustrate.

  1. Long lines are a barrier to voting
  2. This issue affects a large, diverse group of individuals
  3. Convey seriousness, but balanced with optimism

Feedback & Iteration

Our iterations to both the script and video were influenced by the feedback we received from crits:

  • Expand the value of our solution to a community level
  • Adjust the timing to provide more air time
  • Pay attention to Verbal and written nuances in our narration and title page
  • Include NoLineBer references
